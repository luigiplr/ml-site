About Loi Krathong and Yi Peng

When

Loi Krahong occurs every year on the full moon of the 12th Thai month. This year it will be officially held on November 25, and next year it will be on November 14th, but celebrations over the course of several days. The actual dates of the two Yi Peng ceremonies that occur durring these celebrations are not announced until a few weeks beforehand.

Where

Loi Krathong celebrations are held all over Thailand, but the famous Yi Peng celebrations are held in Chiang Mai.

Origins

It is believed that Loy Krathong is an ancient Brahmanic or Indic festival. Originally it was a ceremony where people paid their respects their gods. People would make lanterns using candles and paper, which would then be displayed in the homes of royalty, rich people or high-ranking officials. 150 years ago, at the urging of King Mongkut (Rama IV), it was later adopted by Buddhists as a ceremony to honor the Buddha.

Why You Should Go

The Yi Peng Lantern release is a sight to behold! If you thought the scene from Tangled was spectacular, then you must come see the real thing! Thousands of lanrterns are released into the air, making for an exceptionally breath-taking experience. During the week end, many events are held in the city of Chiang Mai: traditional Thai dance, Grand Yi Peng Parade, beauty pageants, fireworks, decorations in the old city.
