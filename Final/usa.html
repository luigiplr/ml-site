About Mardi Gras and Carnival

When

Mardi Gras is held on, you guessed it, Fat Tuesday. However, due to the extravagent Carnival parades and events that take place in the days before, it is advised that you arrive on Sunday to avoid missing anything exciting! In 2016 Fat Tuesday will be on Febuary 9th.

Where

There are Mardi Gras celebrations and parades all over the world, but the most memorable are in New Orleans, Louisiana. While a lot of the fun happens in the French Quarter, don't forget to check out the parades in Uptown New Orleans and Mid-City.

Origins

Mardi Gras is French for "Fat Tuesday", reflecting the practice of the last night of eating richer, fatty foods before the ritual fasting of the Lenten season. While it has always been reason to celebrate, the earliest reference to New Orleans Mardi Gras "Carnival" appears in 1781. Since then, the Carnival has grown to include the iconic masks, flashy parades and exotic costumes that makes this festival so well-known today.

Why You Should Go

This famous two-week festival features parades headed by ‘Kings’ and ‘Queens’ leading a flotilla of garish floats manned by ‘krewes’ who throw trinkets to the crowds. Bands play, booze is drunk, the partying escalates - the culmination is the wicked mayhem of Mardi Gras Day, when all inhibitions are let loose. A crazy celebration before lent, aka the last day before giving up sinful pleasure.
