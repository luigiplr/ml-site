About Holi

When

In most of India, Holi is celebrated the day after the full moon in March each year. (However, it's celebrated a day earlier in some parts of eastern India, such as the state of West Bengal). This means that in 2015, it was be celebrated on March 6, but in 2016, it will be celebrated on March 23.

Where

While more and more Holi festivals are being held outside India (even people at UVic were covered in paint on Marck 3rd) the best festivals remain in the land of its origin. While there are many variations, some stand out. There is an awesome festival in Mathura at the Holi Gate, a week-long celebration in Vrindavan, and, for those who want a more main-stream celebration, the Holi Cow festival in Delhi.

Origins

Holi celebrations have been referenced in texts from as early as 300 BC. It is a Hindu fesival that celebrates coming of spring and the victory of good over evil. The literal meaning of the word 'Holi' is 'burning'. There are various legends to explain the meaning of this word, most prominent of all is the legend associated with demon king Hiranyakashyap.

Why You Should Go

Indian Holi Festival is a once-a-year event renowned for fun and ubiquitous color powder. It is one of the best occasions to experience India at its happiest. Honestly, who could say no to multi-colored powder throwing, live music performed by indie musicians, street food, and madness?
